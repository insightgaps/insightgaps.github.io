Methods

How We Work

Forensic data journalism requires precision above speed, verification above volume. These are the standards we apply to every investigation.

01 // Core Principle

InsightGaps only publishes what can be verified through primary source documentation or multiple independent corroborating sources. When the data shows something surprising, that is reason to dig harder — not reason to publish faster.

We do not print unverified allegations as fact. We distinguish between what the documents show, what sources say, and what our analysis concludes. Readers can always trace our path.

02 // Data Collection

We build original datasets from primary sources, not from secondary reporting. Our sources include:

  • Government records obtained through Right to Information (RTI) requests
  • Court case files, FIR records, and judicial statistics
  • One-Stop Crisis Centre (OSCC) administrative data
  • NGO and advocacy organisation case registers
  • Named on-record interviews with lawyers, doctors, and advocates
  • Direct interviews with survivors where consent is explicit and ongoing

03 // Evidence Tiers

Every factual claim in our investigations is coded to one of three evidence tiers:

Confirmed

Documented in primary source materials and cross-verified by at least two independent sources. Used for all headline figures.

Corroborated

Stated by a named source and consistent with other evidence. We note this is sourced, not document-verified.

Unverified

Reported elsewhere or stated by anonymous source. Not used in headline claims. Noted only for context.

04 // Statistical Standards

Rates and percentages are calculated using the most conservative methodology available. Where data has gaps (missing years, incomplete records), we note the gap and do not extrapolate unless the extrapolation is clearly labelled as an estimate with the method stated.

We prefer undercounting to overcounting. If the real number is worse than our figure, the direction of our finding holds. If counting methods differ between sources, we use the primary administrative record as the baseline.

05 // Source Protection

Survivors and vulnerable sources are never named without explicit, informed, and ongoing consent. All names used in published work are either given with full consent or have been anonymised. We do not use court records to identify survivors in ways that go beyond what official processes have already made public.

Named institutional sources — lawyers, officials, advocates — are quoted directly and given right of reply before publication.

06 // Corrections Policy

If a published figure is subsequently shown to be incorrect, we issue a correction at the top of the relevant page, note the original figure and the corrected one, and explain how the error occurred. We do not silently update records. Corrections are permanent and dated.

To report a factual error: contact us via the About page.
