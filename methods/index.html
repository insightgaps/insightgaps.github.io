Methods & Standards

How Insight Gaps investigates systemic failures — the evidence standards, source hierarchy, statistical methods, and editorial rules that govern every claim we publish.

Last updated: March 2026

The credibility of forensic data journalism depends entirely on its methodology. Every finding in every investigation is only as strong as the evidence behind it. We document our methods openly — so that our findings can be challenged, reproduced, and built upon by anyone.

Evidence Tier System

Every factual claim in every Insight Gaps investigation carries one of four evidence tier labels. These are not rhetorical qualifications — they are a structured declaration of the chain of evidence behind each claim. Readers and researchers can assess our confidence in any individual finding without reading the full methodology document.

CONFIRMED — Established with high confidence from primary or official sources. Requires: (a) two or more independent primary sources; or (b) one official source with corroborating evidence; or (c) a court record, UN treaty body document, or directly cited official statistic with an accessible URL. Claims at this tier can be verified by any third party from the cited source alone.

PROBABLE — Likely accurate but does not meet the CONFIRMED threshold. Requires one named primary source plus a corroborating pattern from CONFIRMED data, or a calculation derived from two or more CONFIRMED inputs where the derivation logic is fully stated. Includes modelled estimates where all inputs are CONFIRMED. The model itself is disclosed.

ALLEGED — Reported by credible journalists or named witnesses but not yet confirmed by primary documentation. Used sparingly. The investigation is actively working to upgrade this tier or has documented why upgrading is not currently possible.

CONFIRMED ABSENT — A data point that should exist but demonstrably does not. An active search across three or more institutional channels returns no result. The absence itself is documented as an accountability finding — it records that the responsible institution does not publish data it is capable of holding. This is not a research limitation. It is evidence of a governance failure.

Source Hierarchy

Sources are weighted in the following order. Higher-ranked sources override lower-ranked sources where they conflict. Conflicts are documented, not suppressed.

  • Rank 1: Official government primary documents — PHQ crime statistics, Supreme Court annual reports, ministry data, parliamentary records.
  • Rank 2: UN treaty body documents and UNSG submissions — CEDAW reports, UPR submissions, UN special rapporteur findings.
  • Rank 3: Peer-reviewed academic research with accessible DOIs and disclosed methodology.
  • Rank 4: Major INGO programme evaluations — BRAC, UN Women, HRW, Amnesty International — with named data collection methodology.
  • Rank 5: Named national NGO monitoring reports — Naripokkho, BLAST, ASK, Bangladesh Jatri Kalyan Samity — with disclosed methodology.
  • Rank 6: National press of record — Daily Star, Prothom Alo, TBS News, bdnews24, BSS — named reporter, named source, accessible URL.
  • Rank 7: International press coverage of Bangladesh — The Guardian, Reuters, AP — for context and cross-reference only, never as sole primary evidence.

No anonymous source is used as supporting evidence for any primary claim. Unnamed sources may appear in case notes as context. They are never cited as evidence for a tiered finding.

Statistical and Modelling Standards

Where investigations include compound probability models, rate calculations, or derived estimates, the following rules apply without exception:

  • Every input to every model is disclosed with its source and evidence tier.
  • Sensitivity analysis is run across the full documented range of each input. The direction of the finding must be stable across all variants for a claim to proceed.
  • Models are labelled PROBABLE regardless of input tier, because estimation is introduced at the compound stage.
  • Conservative inputs are preferred. Where a range exists, we use the floor, not the ceiling, unless the floor would understate attrition — in which case the methodological choice is documented.
  • Government statistics that misrepresent a measurement are corrected formally. The correction names the source, the misrepresentation, and the correct interpretation. We do not silently substitute a corrected figure.

Data Sources Used in Published Investigations

Insight Gaps investigations draw on primary public data. All sources are cited in full within investigation reports and listed in the evidence tier tables. Source categories used include:

  • Government administrative data — Bangladesh Police Headquarters (PHQ) crime statistics, BRTA road safety data, Bangladesh Meteorological Department records, official census and survey data.
  • International institutional data — WHO Global Status Reports, IMF World Economic Outlook, World Bank datasets, FAO commodity data, UN population and development surveys.
  • National survey data — BBS/UNFPA Violence Against Women Survey, Bangladesh Demographic and Health Survey, CIPRB National Injury Survey.
  • Court and legal records — tribunal case registers, High Court cause lists, and documented case timelines from named court reporters.
  • Programme evaluation data — NGO monitoring reports, INGO programme outcomes data, development organisation assessments.
  • Press of record — documented media reports with named sources, used for corroboration and case tracking, never as sole evidence.

Handling Source Conflicts

Where two credible sources report conflicting figures for the same data point, the conflict is documented in the methodology and in the investigation itself — not resolved by choosing the more convenient figure. Both figures appear with their sources and a documented explanation of the most likely cause of divergence (different denominators, different time periods, different methodology). The investigation uses the more conservative figure and labels it PROBABLE until the conflict is resolved. All unresolved conflicts are logged.

Documenting Absence as Evidence

Where data that should exist in the public record is absent, the absence is treated as a finding. An active search is conducted across three or more institutional sources. If no result is returned, the absence is logged as CONFIRMED ABSENT with the search record documented. The government institution that holds or should hold the data is named. Data absence is never treated as a research limitation — it is a documented governance failure.

This approach is applied consistently in BD-INV-002: the complaint-to-FIR conversion rate, the charge-sheet-to-trial rate, and the district-level conviction breakdown are all documented as CONFIRMED ABSENT — not as unknowns, but as data the state chooses not to publish.

Editorial Independence

  • No corporate funding. No advertising. No paywalls on public investigations.
  • No findings for sale. Analytical conclusions are derived from data. They are not shaped by any external relationship or funding source.
  • No organisation is exempt. We do not withhold, modify, or soften findings to protect any government, NGO, institution, or individual.
  • Proportionality. Findings are stated in proportion to the evidence. We do not overstate certainty or extrapolate beyond what the data supports. Where we are uncertain, we say so precisely.

Open Access and Licensing

All public investigations are published under Creative Commons Attribution Non-Commercial 4.0 (CC BY-NC 4.0). This means anyone — researchers, journalists, policymakers, students — can reproduce, share, and build on our findings for non-commercial purposes, provided the source is credited and evidence tier labels are preserved when citing specific claims.

Raw data files (leads.json, cases.json, monthly.json) are published alongside investigations under the same licence. Tier labels are mandatory when citing individual data points.

Corrections Policy

If a factual error is identified in any published work, it will be corrected promptly. The correction is noted in the document with the date of amendment and a plain description of what was changed and why. We do not silently edit published reports. Version history is maintained on GitHub for all investigations.
