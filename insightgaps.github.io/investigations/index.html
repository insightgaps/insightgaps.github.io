The Impunity Machine: Bangladesh's Crisis of Justice for Sexual Violence

A forensic audit of Bangladesh's rape justice system. 310 convictions in 23 years for 66,711 women who reached a One-Stop Crisis Centre. Zero convictions in the first seven months of 2024. The report documents the structural chain producing this outcome: silence, shalish, forensic failure, delay, acquittal.

Primary Findings
  • Machine-level impunity: Fewer than 2 convictions per 10,000 sexual assaults on mid-scenario probability modelling.
  • India recalculation: India's published 22.7% rate drops to ~2–3% on Bangladesh's denominator — the systems are structurally identical.
  • Death penalty failure: Three mechanisms by which the 2020 amendment likely worsened, not improved, conviction rates.

An open-source forensic dataset on bus-related fatalities in Dhaka, the structural incentives of the waybill (Joma) system, and the accountability failures that perpetuate them. Six operators documented. Phoenix hypothesis: banned operators rebranding to evade enforcement.

Primary Findings
  • The Data Gap: Official figures undercount fatalities by approximately 5.8× vs WHO estimates.
  • Phoenix Phenomenon: Banned operators (e.g. Suprobhat) rebrand as Victor Classic and Akash to bypass enforcement.
  • Economic Incentives: The "Joma" system creates rational incentives for bus racing, directly causing pedestrian deaths.

The Hidden Tax of War: How Conflicts Raise Global Prices

How armed conflicts — Iraq 2003, Arab Spring 2011, Ukraine 2022, Red Sea 2023–24 — transmit economic shocks through energy, food, and shipping markets to countries that never participated. Introduces the War Externality Index (WEI) framework for comparative cross-conflict analysis.

Primary Findings
  • One repeated transmission mechanism across multiple conflicts drives global price spillovers to uninvolved nations.
  • Import-dependent countries absorb disproportionate welfare losses — bottom-quintile households lose 3–5% of real income.
  • The WEI framework enables cross-conflict quantitative comparison of economic externalities.
