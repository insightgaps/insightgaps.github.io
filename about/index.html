InsightGaps

Bangladesh's first dedicated data journalism portal — independent, unfunded, and structurally incapable of bias.

InsightGaps is Bangladesh's first dedicated data journalism portal. Not a newspaper. Not an NGO bulletin. Not a funded research project. A bureau built entirely around one method: take a question that matters, find every piece of primary source data that touches it, build a structured dataset, visualise it rigorously, and publish everything — including the gaps, the conflicts, and the things the data cannot yet answer.

Bangladesh has no shortage of journalism. It has a shortage of data journalism — the kind that builds original datasets from court records, government filings, RTI responses, and primary NGO registers, then makes those datasets publicly available for anyone to check, challenge, or extend. That gap is what this bureau exists to fill.

What makes this different from traditional reporting: every claim carries an evidence tier label. Every figure shows its source, its methodology, and its limitations. Conflicting data from different sources is published alongside each other, not resolved by editorial preference. The full underlying dataset — not just the article — is always available for download. Findings must be reproducible. If you can't replicate a number from our published sources, that is a correction request, not a disagreement.

What makes this different from funded research: InsightGaps accepts no institutional funding, no advertising, no sponsorship, and no grants. No donor shapes the coverage. No editorial board approves the topics. The only constraint on what gets investigated is whether the primary source data exists to investigate it responsibly. This is not a political position — it is a methodological one. Funded journalism produces funded conclusions. We produce data.

The investigations use advanced data analysis and interactive visualisation tools — probability models, geographic mapping, live tracking dashboards, heatmaps, and structured evidence registries — because the stories that matter most in Bangladesh are structural stories. They cannot be told with a single quote or a single statistic. They require readers to see the whole system at once. That is what the visualisations are for: not decoration, but argument.

Heisenberg is the founding editor. The name is an alias — chosen deliberately. Werner Heisenberg's uncertainty principle holds that the act of observation changes what is observed. This bureau exists to observe what the state has decided not to measure. The identity of the editor is irrelevant to the validity of the data. Check the sources. The numbers stand or fall on their own.

Coverage
What InsightGaps Investigates
  • Accountability gaps — where official data should exist and doesn't. The absence of data is itself a finding.
  • Criminal justice — conviction rates, backlog, forensic infrastructure, legal architecture failures.
  • Gender-based violence — structural, systemic, data-driven. Not individual cases — the system that processes them.
  • Conflict economics — the financial architecture of political violence, war, and state capture.
  • Institutional failure — where state systems produce predictable, documented, structural harm.
  • Global comparators — Bangladesh's systems measured against regional and international equivalents using the same methodology.
Editorial Standards
10 Rules We Follow Without Exception
  • Never publish a figure that isn't tied to a named source or primary document.
  • If the data shows something surprising, dig harder — not an excuse to rush.
  • Every data point is a person. The methodology must be worthy of that.
  • We undercount, not overcount. If the real number is worse, our direction holds.
  • Corrections are permanent, visible, and dated. We do not silently update.
  • Official denial is data, not a rebuttal. We publish both.
  • We do not accept advertising, funding, or money that shapes coverage. Ever.
  • Source protection is non-negotiable. Consent is explicit and ongoing.
  • The full dataset is always available for inspection. Findings must be reproducible.
  • We stop publishing when we can't verify. A gap in publication is better than a gap in fact.
