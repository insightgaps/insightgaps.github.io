About the Bureau

Insight Gaps

An independent public-interest research bureau focused on surfacing hidden operational patterns through forensic data analysis.

Most operational failure is not dramatic. It is quiet, structural, and accumulative — invisible to standard reporting until it has already compounded.

Insight Gaps exists to make those patterns visible. We follow the evidence wherever it leads, document what we find, and publish everything under evidence tier labels so readers can assess our confidence in every claim.

Editorial Independence

No corporate funding. No advertising. No paywalls on core investigations. The Bureau’s analytical conclusions are not for sale — they are derived from data, following the evidence wherever it leads.

We do not accept funding from organizations we investigate or analyze. We do not take positions on behalf of clients in public reporting. Every claim in every investigation carries a source citation and an evidence tier label.

What We Publish

Public investigations are fully open-access forensic reports examining systemic operational issues — available to any reader, journalist, or researcher. All investigations are licensed under Creative Commons BY-NC 4.0.

Applied analysis translates investigative findings into operational intelligence — structured forensic reports on specific domains, published openly and available as the basis for commissioned private engagements.

Evidence Tiers

Every factual claim in every investigation carries one of four evidence tier labels. These are not rhetorical qualifications — they are a structured declaration of the chain of evidence behind each claim.

Confirmed

Primary source documentation. Government data, court records, peer-reviewed studies, official UN/INGO reports with named data collection methodology. The claim can be verified by a third party from the cited source.

Probable

Strongly supported but not independently verifiable from a single primary source. Derived from consistent secondary sources, triangulated data, or model-based calculations disclosed in methodology.

Alleged

Reported by credible journalists or witnesses but not yet confirmed by primary documentation. The bureau is working to upgrade this tier or has documented why upgrading is not currently possible.

Confirmed Absent

Data that should exist but demonstrably does not. The government holds this information. No public record exists. The absence itself is documented as evidence of a governance failure.

Editorial Principles

Principle 01
Follow the Evidence
Conclusions follow from data, never the reverse. We do not begin with a finding and seek supporting evidence.
Principle 02
Acknowledge Uncertainty
Every claim is evidence-tiered. Where we are not certain, we say so precisely — not approximately.
Principle 03
Name the Void
When data the government should hold does not exist in public record, we document the absence as evidence.
Principle 04
Open Source
All public investigations are open-access, CC BY-NC 4.0. Evidence files are published alongside reports.
Principle 05
No Paywalls
Core investigations are fully public. Private commissioned work is funded through direct engagement, not reader paywalls.
Principle 06
Corrections Welcome
Every report carries a correction address. Evidence disputes are processed publicly and version-controlled.

Commissioned Work

The Bureau accepts a limited number of private operational diagnostics for organizations facing complex documentation or workflow failures. These private engagements follow the exact same methodology and independence standards as our public investigations.

Commissioned work is delivered under strict confidentiality. No client names, data, or findings are disclosed without explicit written consent. Private engagements are funded by the commissioning party; they do not influence any public reporting.
