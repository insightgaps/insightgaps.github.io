Methodology Overview

Pattern-first forensic methodology. All claims drawn from named public records: media archives, NGO research (TIB, BJKS, RSF), official statistics (WHO, BRTA, World Bank), and the ARI-BUET official investigation report. No interviews conducted. Evidence tier labels applied to every claim.

Methodology Sections
M-001 Source Catalogue

155+ sources across media archives, NGO databases, government statistics, and academic research. Each source categorized by type, reliability tier, and claim domain.

M-002 Search Protocol

Systematic keyword search across Bangla and English media archives. Date range: 2015–2024. Search terms documented and reproducible.

M-003 Inclusion Criteria

Only sources with named publication, dateline, and verifiable origin included. Anonymous claims excluded unless corroborated by minimum two independent named sources.

M-004 Deduplication

Incident-level deduplication applied to fatality records. Same incident reported by multiple sources counted once. Primary source hierarchy: official records > NGO records > media.

M-005 Phoenix Tracking

Operator rebranding hypothesis tested via BRTA registration records, fleet identification, and route overlap analysis. Confidence tier assigned per operator.

M-006 Evidence Tiers

CONFIRMED — multiple independent named sources. PROBABLE — single strong source + corroboration. ALLEGED — single source, no corroboration. UNVERIFIED — reported but not substantiated.
