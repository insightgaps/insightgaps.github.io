1. Evidence Tier System

Every factual claim in BD-INV-002 carries one of three evidence tier labels. These are formal classifications, not editorial judgments.

TierDefinitionRule for Assignment
CONFIRMED Established with high confidence from primary or official sources. (a) 2+ independent primary sources; OR (b) 1 official source + corroborating evidence; OR (c) court record, UN treaty body document, or directly cited official statistic with accessible URL.
PROBABLE Likely accurate but does not meet the CONFIRMED threshold. 1 named primary source + corroborating pattern from CONFIRMED data. OR: calculation derived from 2+ CONFIRMED inputs where derivation logic is stated. Includes modelled estimates where inputs are CONFIRMED.
CONFIRMED ABSENT A data point that should exist but demonstrably does not. Active search across 3+ institutional channels returns no result. The absence is an accountability finding — it documents that the responsible institution does not publish data it is capable of holding.

Not used: This investigation does not use an ALLEGED tier for any primary claim. No claim appears without a named source. Anonymous claims appear only in case notes, never as supporting evidence.

2. Conviction Rate Methodology

Bangladesh has never published an official WCRPA conviction rate. All rates cited are drawn from independent research, NGO monitoring, and programme evaluations. Twelve independent measurements exist, ranging from 0.12% to 3.66%. This range is real — it reflects genuine methodological differences.

Denominator definitions

  • Cases filed (PHQ): All WCRPA cases formally registered. Broadest denominator — produces lowest apparent rate.
  • Cases disposed: Only cases that received any verdict (including acquittal). Narrower — produces higher apparent rate. The CEDAW 53.52% uses this denominator and is a disposal rate, not a conviction rate.
  • OCC visitors: Women who attended a One-Stop Crisis Centre. 66,711 visitors → 310 convictions = 0.46%.
⚠ CEDAW 53.52% is not a conviction rate. The figure refers to case disposal rate — the proportion of WCRPA cases receiving any verdict, including acquittals. It has been repeatedly misrepresented in media as a conviction rate. This investigation formally documents this error. The CEDAW committee report does not use the word "conviction." 3. Probability of Punishment Model

The headline figure — 0.0215%, approximately 1 in 4,650 — is a compound probability model. It is marked PROBABLE because the inputs, while individually CONFIRMED, involve estimation at multiple funnel stages.

Model: Compound Attrition (IG-MODEL · PROBABLE)
P(punishment | assault) =
  P(disclosure) × P(police | disclosure) × P(charge sheet | police) × P(conviction | disposed)

= 0.38 × 0.27 × 0.59 × 0.0039 = 0.000215 = 0.0215%

Inputs:
· P(disclosure) = 0.38 — UNFPA 2024 · CONFIRMED · S-02
· P(police | disclosure) = 0.27 — UNFPA 2024 Highlights · CONFIRMED · S-26
· P(charge sheet) = 0.59 — 1 minus PBI 41% unproven rate, 2016–2023 · CONFIRMED · S-34
· P(conviction | disposed) = 0.0039 — Naripokkho floor rate (conservative) · CONFIRMED · S-06

Sensitivity check:
· Using BRAC ceiling (0.0366): P = 0.000202 (~1 in 4,950)
· Using OCC rate (0.0046): P = 0.000026 (~1 in 38,000)
· All variants: below 0.03%. Direction of finding is stable across all inputs.

The Naripokkho floor rate is used as the primary input because it applies the broadest post-police-contact denominator. Using BRAC's ceiling would understate attrition, as it only covers cases that reached disposal. This is the methodologically conservative choice.

5. Documented Source Conflicts

Where two credible sources conflict on a specific figure, both are documented in the Master Evidence File (Sheet 2) with the conflict flagged and resolution stated.

Conflict 01 — 2021 rape filings: Prothom Alo Jul 2025 (S-61) cites 6,341 (PHQ). University of Asia Pacific study (S-62) cites 4,762 for the same year. Resolution: 6,341 used for 2021; 4,762 used for 2022. The S-62 figure appears to reference 2022 data. Both remain PROBABLE pending primary PHQ document retrieval.
Conflict 02 — March 2025 backlog: SHARE-Net (S-63) reports 148,314 pending WCRPA cases; Daily Star (S-64) reports 151,317 for the same reference period. Resolution: reported as "approximately 150,000" with both sources cited. Both are CONFIRMED; the difference likely reflects different administrative cutoff dates within the same reporting month.
Conflict 03 — CEDAW 53.52%: Widely cited as a conviction rate in Bangladeshi media and some NGO reports. This investigation formally reclassifies it as a disposal rate. The underlying CEDAW document uses the term "completion of proceedings," not "conviction." This is documented as the most consequential data misrepresentation in existing coverage of this topic.

6. Confirmed Absent Data — The 12 Gaps

These are not research limitations. They are documented accountability failures. Each represents a dataset that a specific institution holds and does not publish.

GA-001
Annual WCRPA conviction rates by year, tribunal, and district — PHQ holds 25 years of data. Never published. Would immediately confirm or refute the judicial reluctance hypothesis following the 2020 death penalty amendment.
GA-002
FIR registration rate for rape complaints — What percentage of women who approach police receive a formal FIR? This stage is completely unmeasured. It may be where the largest share of attrition occurs.
GA-003
90-day trial timeline compliance rate — The 2025 Ordinance mandates 90-day trials. No institution tracks compliance. The 180-day mandate that preceded it was violated in effectively every case for 25 years.
GA-004
National count of Section 17 prosecutions 2000–2026 — Two cases are documented. The national total is unknown. Under the 2025 Amendment Ordinance's expansion of §17 to "loss of dignity," monitoring is now urgent.
GA-005
WCRPA case withdrawal and abandonment rate — The scale of shalish-driven attrition is completely unmeasured. This is likely the largest single funnel leak and it is invisible to any accountability mechanism.
GA-006
Pre/post 2020 death penalty conviction rate comparison — Five years of post-amendment data exists inside PHQ systems. One published table would resolve the judicial reluctance hypothesis with empirical evidence.
GA-007
DNA processing time by case type and district — 789 cases stalled in September 2025. 92% of samples unprocessed. NFDPL holds the data. Zero public disclosure.
GA-008
Witness Protection Law — Bill drafted. Pending since 2015. Conviction requires witness. Witness requires protection. Four governments. No enactment.
GA-009
Marital rape exemption — CEDAW required removal in 2016. Still in force in 2026. Creates an entire category of legally invisible violence affecting an estimated 76% of women who experience lifetime IPV from partners.
GA-010
District-level WCRPA conviction data — National averages mask geographic variation. PHQ data would reveal whether specific districts or tribunals perform dramatically above or below average.
GA-011
WCRPA disposal vs conviction breakdown — Supreme Court annual reports give total disposals. The split between acquittals and convictions within disposals is not published separately.
GA-012
Section 32 violation tracking — WCRPA §32 mandates hospitals examine rape survivors without requiring a prior police case. Hospital refusals are a documented pattern (HRCBM PIL 2025). No institution tracks compliance.

7. Visual Investigation Methodology

The visual investigation (visual.html) uses scroll-triggered animated data visualisations. All figures displayed in the visual are sourced identically to the main investigation. Specific methodological notes:

  • Dot matrix (Scene 01): 10,000 dots represent an estimated annual non-partner violence cohort (PROBABLE · IG-MODEL). The attrition percentages at each step are derived from UNFPA 2024 (disclosure, police contact) and PBI data (charge sheet closure rate).
  • Clock animation (Scene 02): 56 days = Runa Akhter §17 case (arrest to sentencing · CONFIRMED · S-14). 2,349 days = BRAC/Agile 2022 average verdict time, 16 districts, 385 cases (CONFIRMED · S-01). Arc proportions reflect the same justice scale — the gold arc is 41.9× larger than the red arc.
  • Race bars (Scene 04): Square-root scale is used for visual readability. At linear scale, the Bangladesh bar would be less than 1 pixel wide. The scale choice is labeled explicitly in the visualisation. Exact percentages are labeled on each bar. The denominator alert is displayed before the bars appear.
  • IPV map (Scene 06): Division-level IPV rates are from UNFPA/BBS VAW Survey 2024 (S-74, S-02). Corrected from v1.0 (which contained errors from an earlier dataset). All v2.0 values are CONFIRMED.
  • Scatter plot (Scene 08): Country positioning on severity axis is ordinal, not interval — it represents relative severity of maximum penalty, not a precise scale. Country conviction rates use the denominator specified in the source for each country.

8. Live Record Methodology

The live record (Section 10 of the visual investigation, and the dashboard in the main investigation) is updated via a GitHub Actions automated collector running every 6 hours. The collector scrapes named RSS feeds from Bangladeshi news sources, classifies items using Gemini Flash, deduplicates against existing leads, and appends new items to /data/leads.json.

Tier assignment in automated collection: The collector assigns an initial tier of UNVERIFIED to all automated items. Items are upgraded to PROBABLE or CONFIRMED only after manual cross-reference check. No automated item reaches the investigation narrative without human verification.

Case register: Cases in /data/cases.json are manually maintained with full timelines and source URLs. Each timeline event carries its own tier label. Case status is updated when new information is confirmed.

Monthly ledger: /data/monthly.json distinguishes between CONFIRMED data (primary PHQ sources), PROBABLE data (secondary citing PHQ), and CONFIRMED ABSENT months (where PHQ does not publish monthly breakdowns). The CONFIRMED ABSENT entries are accountability findings — each red cell in the heatmap represents a month for which a government institution holds data and does not publish it.
