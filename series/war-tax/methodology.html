The Hidden Tax of War

Methodology Overview

Quantitative cross-conflict methodology. Four conflicts analyzed across energy, food, and shipping transmission channels. War Externality Index (WEI) constructed to enable consistent cross-conflict comparison. All data drawn from World Bank, FAO, UNCTAD, and IMF public datasets.

Methodology Sections
M-001 Conflict Selection

Four conflicts selected for geographic, temporal, and mechanism diversity: Iraq 2003 (energy), Arab Spring 2011 (food/political), Ukraine 2022 (energy/food/shipping), Red Sea 2023–24 (shipping). Selection criteria documented.

M-002 Transmission Channels

Three primary transmission channels defined: energy price shock propagation, food commodity price transmission, shipping route disruption. Each channel modeled separately then combined.

M-003 WEI Construction

War Externality Index constructed as composite measure: (energy shock × weight) + (food shock × weight) + (shipping disruption × weight). Weights derived from import dependency ratios for target country class.

M-004 Welfare Impact

Bottom-quintile household welfare loss estimated using import share of consumption basket. 3–5% real income loss figure derived from import dependency and price transmission coefficients.

M-005 Country Classification

Countries classified by conflict participation (direct, indirect, uninvolved) and import dependency (low, medium, high). Analysis focuses on uninvolved high-import-dependency countries.

M-006 Data Sources

World Bank commodity price indices (primary). FAO food price data. UNCTAD shipping rate records. IMF balance-of-payments. All series publicly accessible and cited.
