The Lead Belt

44 unregulated lead battery recycling sites operate within 500 metres of 145 schools across Bangladesh. The nearest contaminated site is 59 metres from a primary school in Kamrangir Char, Dhaka. Soil lead at that site measures 330,000 ppm — 825 times the safe limit set by the US EPA. The Department of Environment has documented this contamination since 2016. Zero facilities have been permanently closed by regulatory action.

  • ~39,875 students attend schools within 500 metres of a confirmed contaminated site today.
  • 38% of Bangladeshi children carry blood lead levels above the threshold for irreversible neurological harm. In Dhaka, the figure is 65%. There is no safe treatment for lead poisoning.
  • 22 years of documentation. 2003 to 2025. Not one ULAB facility permanently closed by regulatory action. The Bangladesh Bureau of Statistics catalogued 12,207 battery recycling operations in 2003. The number has grown.

Editorial Standards

How We Work

// Evidence Tiers
Confirmed Verified through two or more independent primary sources. Laboratory results, official records, or government documentation.
Probable Supported by primary sources with minor gaps. High confidence, explicit uncertainty noted.
Alleged Single source or secondhand documentation. Published for public interest; not independently verified.
Unverified Reported claim without primary documentation. Included for context only; not treated as established fact.
// Our Rules

Every statistic is tied to a named source or primary document. If the data shows something surprising, we dig harder — not faster.

We undercount, not overcount. If the real figure is worse, our direction holds. Corrections are permanent, visible, and dated.

Official denial is data, not a rebuttal. We publish both. We do not accept advertising or funding that shapes coverage.

The full dataset is always available for inspection. Findings must be reproducible →
