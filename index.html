The Impunity Machine:
Bangladesh’s Rape Justice Crisis

310 convictions in 23 years for 66,711 women who came forward. Zero convictions in the first seven months of 2024. A forensic audit of why Bangladesh’s rape justice system produces impunity with the consistency of a machine.

Full Report → Visual Story → Methodology
01

0.46% conviction rate: Of 66,711 women who reached a crisis centre (2001–Jul 2024), only 310 resulted in conviction. The government has never published an official rate.

02

Zero convictions Jan–Jul 2024: 643 women sought help. 227 cases filed. 12 verdicts. Not one conviction — while the same system convicted the woman who reported in 56 days.

03

+52% surge in 2025: Bangladesh Police recorded 786 rape victims H1 2025 vs 516 the previous year. Child rape up 75%. The machine accelerating while conviction rates stay flat.

04

21-year verdict: Jamalpur 2004–2025 — the longest documented case. At current disposal rate, clearing the backlog would take 26+ years with zero new filings.
