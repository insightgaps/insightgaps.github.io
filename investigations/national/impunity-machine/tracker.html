4,516,603 cases pending nationally as of January 10, 2026 — rape backlog sits inside a system near collapse
The Supreme Court Secretariat (established December 2025) published the first independent judicial backlog figure. Lower courts hold 86% of all pending cases. The ~150,000 WCRPA backlog is a subset of a system-wide crisis. Even if rape tribunals were instantly reformed, the surrounding infrastructure cannot process the volume.

Marital rape is not criminalised under WCRPA. The majority of this violence is legally invisible regardless of tribunal capacity.

01
Forensic Access — 72-Hour Guarantee
Every district should have a trained forensic physician and OSCC access. 78% of survivors currently arrive after the 72-hour evidence window. 50 of 64 districts have no full OCC.
Committed by
GoB UNSG submission May 2024 — 14 OCCs operational

02
Witness Protection Law
Bill drafted 2015. Not enacted as of March 2026. Witnesses are unprotected. Conviction requires witness testimony. The logical chain is broken by deliberate legislative inaction across four governments.
Bill status
Draft exists since 2015 — 11 years unlegislated

03
90-Day Trial Mandate — Enforcement
The 90-day deadline exists in law. It has never been enforced. The Barishal Trish Godown case (February 2026) missed the deadline by 3,290 days. No tribunal has ever faced sanction for missing the 90-day requirement.
Law status
Exists in WCRPA. Enforcement mechanism: absent
Unenforced

DNA Laboratory Capacity
2 machines for 170 million people. 92% of 2025 samples unprocessed. 789 cases stalled. The government has acknowledged the backlog. No additional machine procurement has been confirmed or funded.

Annual Official Conviction Rate Publication
PHQ, MoLJPA, and MoWCA have never published an annual official conviction rate for rape cases. This tracker exists because no official version exists. The government cannot be held accountable for a rate it refuses to calculate.

Section 17 was expanded in the 2025 Amendment Ordinance to include "loss of dignity or reputation." This is a documented regression. The reform needed is the reverse: restrict §17 to require independent judicial pre-authorization before any counter-prosecution can be filed.

NFDPL · National Forensic DNA Profiling Lab
DNA Backlog Status
The single hardest mechanical bottleneck. Updated when new NFDPL data is published.
Samples unprocessed (2025)
92%
1,204 of 1,304 samples received in 2025 remain unprocessed as of September 2025. Processing rate: approximately 8 cases per month. At current rate, clearing the 2025 backlog alone would take over a year — without any new samples arriving.
Source: Prothom Alo Bangla · September 2025 · CONFIRMED · S-66
Last updated: Sep 2025 — update when new NFDPL data published
DNA machines serving 170 million people
2
One national DNA profiling lab (NFDPL, Dhaka) plus 5 regional screening labs that cannot produce final court reports. All final forensic reports must route through NFDPL. No second profiling lab exists outside Dhaka. Average processing time: 3–4 months per case.
Source: BRAC 2022 (S-01) · MoWCA data 2025 · CONFIRMED
Cases stalled nationally (Sep 2025)
789
789 active prosecutions cannot proceed to trial because their DNA forensic reports have not been issued. These are not cold cases — they are live cases with waiting survivors and defendants where the system is the bottleneck. Updated when new figures published.
Source: Prothom Alo Bangla · September 2025 · CONFIRMED
NFDPL court report issuance rate (since 2006)
55%
Of 11,501 cases evaluated by NFDPL since 2006, only 6,333 resulted in a court report that could be used in prosecution. 45% of all DNA evidence submitted never reaches the courtroom — before the backlog is even counted.
Source: MoWCA NFDPL data 2025 · CONFIRMED
The mechanical chain: No forensic report → case cannot go to trial → case joins backlog → backlog grows → 789 cases already stalled → each new rape case adds to queue. The conviction rate cannot improve through any legal or judicial reform while this bottleneck exists. This is not a secondary issue. It is the floor on which everything else rests.
How This Works
What This Tracker Is — and Isn't
What this is
A monitoring station. It tracks one question: is anything changing?

The full historical argument — 23 years of data, global comparisons, deterrence theory, geographic breakdown — is in the Data Investigation. That case is made. This page tracks what happens next.

Sources: BMP monthly press conferences, PHQ quarterly data, OSCC aggregates, named press publications for individual case developments. Updated when something changes, not on a fixed schedule.
What this isn't
A complete case registry. Media covers an estimated 2–5% of actual rape incidents. BMP and PHQ data are themselves undercounts. Every figure here is the floor, not the ceiling.

This tracker does not replace court records, police station data, or tribunal registers — none of which are publicly accessible. The gap between what appears here and the real numbers is itself the story.

The data exists somewhere. No institution publishes it. That confirmed absence is the finding.
