The Inversion — Section 17

The system does not fail women equally across the board. In documented cases, it convicts the woman who reports faster than it convicts the man she reported. Two confirmed cases are documented. The national scale is unknown — because no institution tracks this. That absence is itself the finding.

What Section 17 is
Section 17 of the Women and Children Repression Prevention Act 2000 allows counter-prosecution of anyone who files a rape complaint later deemed false or motivated. The 2025 Amendment Ordinance expanded the grounds to include "loss of dignity or reputation" — creating a documented expansion of risk without any monitoring mechanism. Two confirmed cases are documented below. No institution tracks §17 prosecutions nationally — meaning the chilling effect on reporting cannot be quantified. This is a confirmed absence, not a research gap.

// Five structural failures driving the 0.46%
01 · Forensic Bottleneck
92% of DNA samples unprocessed — 1,204 of 1,304 pending as of Sep 2025. 2 machines for 170 million people.
02 · Timeline Collapse
Average verdict: 2,349 days. Legal mandate: 180 days. Compliance rate: effectively zero for 25 years.
03 · Shalish / Withdrawal
Village arbitration pressure causes case withdrawal before trial. National scale cannot be quantified — data does not exist.
04 · No Witness Protection
Bangladesh has no witness protection law. Draft bill exists. Not enacted. Regional outlier.
05 · Marital Rape Exemption
WCRPA still exempts marital rape (wife over 13). 75.9% of women experience lifetime IPV — mostly by spouse. Majority of violence is legally invisible.

Barishal Trish Godown case: 4 death sentences — crime from November 2016 (3,380 days)
Four accused received death sentences for a November 2016 assault. From crime to verdict: approximately 3,380 days — 9.3 years. This is the documented pattern: the cases that receive verdicts take nearly a decade.
Source: BSS February 2026 · CONFIRMED · S-70
January 2026 · Supreme Court Secretariat
4,516,603 cases pending nationally as of January 10, 2026
The Supreme Court Secretariat (established December 2025) published the first independent judicial backlog figure: 4.5 million cases nationwide. Lower courts hold 86% of the total. The WCRPA backlog of ~150,000 sits inside a system already near collapse.
Source: Lawyers Club BD via SC Secretariat · CONFIRMED · S-76
October 2025 · UNFPA/BBS Survey
76% of women experience lifetime intimate partner violence — Barishal highest at 81.5%
The BBS/UNFPA Violence Against Women Survey (27,476 women) found 76% lifetime IPV nationally. 49% experienced violence in the past 12 months. 62% told no one. Most of this violence is legally invisible — marital rape is not criminalised under WCRPA.
Source: BBS/UNFPA VAW Survey October 2025 · CONFIRMED · S-74
September 2025 · DNA Lab Crisis
92% of DNA samples unprocessed — 1,204 of 1,304 samples pending nationwide
The National Forensic DNA Profiling Laboratory has 1,204 unprocessed samples from 1,304 received. Processing rate: 8%. Only 2 functioning DNA analysis machines serve the entire country of 170 million. Without forensic evidence, conviction rates cannot improve regardless of legal reform.
Source: Prothom Alo Bangla · CONFIRMED · S-66

// Methodology & Data Notes

All data in this tracker originates from: One-Stop Crisis Centres (OSCC) 2001–2024 (primary source), Bangladesh Women Journalists' Forum (BWJF) court records, judicial annual reports, and cross-verification with 91 named sources including lawyers, doctors, and survivors' advocates.

The conviction rate (0.46%) is calculated as confirmed convictions ÷ total women who reached crisis centres and sought formal help. The denominator does not include unreported cases (estimated significantly higher). The CEDAW figure of 53.52% is a disposal rate — the proportion of cases receiving any verdict, including acquittals. It has been repeatedly misrepresented in media as a conviction rate. This tracker formally documents that error.

The compound probability model (0.0215% / 1 in 4,650) is marked PROBABLE: each input is individually CONFIRMED, but the compound model involves estimation across four funnel stages. Using BRAC's ceiling rate produces 1 in 4,950; using OCC rate produces 1 in 38,000. All variants are below 0.03%. The direction is stable under all inputs.

Crisis counter (page header): estimated rapes calculated at 13/day (PHQ CONFIRMED, S-16), counted from 1 January 2026. This is a conservative estimate. Unreported cases are not included.
