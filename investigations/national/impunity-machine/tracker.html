// Data Methodology

All data in this tracker originates from: One-Stop Crisis Centres (OSCC) across Bangladesh (primary source, 2001–2024), Bangladesh Women Journalists' Forum (BWJF) court records, judicial annual reports, and cross-verification with 91 named sources including lawyers, doctors, and survivors' advocates.

Conviction rate is calculated as confirmed convictions ÷ total women who reached crisis centres and sought formal help. The denominator does not include unreported cases, which are estimated to be significantly higher. "Pending" cases are those filed but without a final verdict as of the data cutoff.

The +52% surge figure (H1 2025) compares January–June 2025 vs January–June 2024. Child rape data is separately coded from the main rape category per the Women and Child Repression Prevention Act 2000.

This tracker will be updated as new data becomes available. All figures are the most conservative (lowest) available estimate where ranges exist. Full methodology: read the methods note.

March 2026 · BMP Press Conference
442 violence cases in March 2026 — 125 girls + 38 women raped; 36 gang-rape victims
Bangladesh Mahila Parishad (BMP) documented 442 violence cases in March 2026. Of these: 125 girls raped, 38 women raped, 36 victims of gang rape. The pattern continues the +52% surge from H1 2025.
Source: News on Air via BMP · PROBABLE · S-73b
February 2026 · Barishal Verdict
Barishal Trish Godown case: 4 death sentences — case from November 2016 (3,380 days)
Four accused received death sentences for a November 2016 assault. From crime to verdict: approximately 3,380 days — 9.3 years. This is the documented pattern: the cases that do receive verdicts take nearly a decade.
Source: BSS February 2026 · CONFIRMED · S-70
January 2026 · SC Secretariat Data
4,516,603 cases pending nationally as of January 10, 2026
The Supreme Court Secretariat (established December 2025) published the first independent judicial backlog figure: 4.5 million cases nationwide. Lower courts hold 86% of the total. The rape/WCRPA backlog of ~150,000 sits inside a system already near collapse.
Source: Lawyers Club BD via SC Secretariat · CONFIRMED · S-76
October 2025 · UNFPA/BBS Survey
76% of women experience lifetime intimate partner violence — highest Barishal at 81.5%
The BBS/UNFPA Violence Against Women Survey (October 2025, 27,476 women) found 76% lifetime IPV nationally. 49% experienced violence in the past 12 months. 62% told no one. Barishal: 81.5%. Khulna: 81.3%. Most of this violence is legally invisible — marital rape is not criminalised under WCRPA.
Source: BBS/UNFPA VAW Survey October 2025 · CONFIRMED · S-74
September 2025 · DNA Lab Crisis
92% of DNA samples unprocessed — 1,204 of 1,304 samples pending
The National Forensic DNA Profiling Laboratory has 1,204 unprocessed samples from 1,304 total received. Processing rate: 8%. Only 2 functioning DNA analysis machines serve the entire country of 170 million people. Without forensic evidence, conviction rates cannot improve regardless of legal reform.
Source: Prothom Alo Bangla · CONFIRMED · S-66

What Section 17 is

Section 17 of the Women and Children Repression Prevention Act 2000 allows counter-prosecution of anyone who files a rape complaint that is later deemed false or motivated. Under the 2025 Amendment Ordinance, the grounds were expanded to include "loss of dignity or reputation" — creating a documented risk of expansion.

Two confirmed cases are documented below. No institution tracks §17 prosecutions nationally — meaning the chilling effect on reporting cannot be quantified. This is a confirmed absence, not a research gap.
