Scene 08 · The Paradox

Maximum Severity. Near-Zero Certainty.

Criminological consensus: certainty deters crime. Severity alone does not. Bangladesh chose severity. Watch where it places them.

Nagin 2013 · Dölling 2009 · Abramovaite 2022 · NPA 2023/24 · CPS 2024 · NCRB 2022 · OCC 2024
Step 01 of 03

Criminological consensus: certainty is the primary deterrent.

Nagin (2013): certainty of apprehension — not severity of punishment — is the primary deterrent to crime. Dölling et al. (2009) meta-analysis: deterrence hypothesis "rarely confirmed" for serious offences. This is not a minority view. It is the 50-year consensus across jurisdictions.

Academic consensus · Cross-jurisdictional · No credible counter-evidence for rape specifically
Step 02 of 03

Countries with higher certainty achieve higher conviction rates.

South Africa: high certainty (forensic infrastructure, integrated support) → 72.2% conviction rate. No death penalty for rape. Indonesia: implemented death penalty for child rape → documented post-implementation surge in child rape rates. The mechanism is known. Certainty deters. Severity alone does not.

Susila 2019 · S-52 · CONFIRMED
Step 03 of 03

Bangladesh is in the worst possible corner.

Maximum severity: mandatory death penalty since October 2020. Near-zero certainty: 0.46% conviction rate. The data places Bangladesh exactly where the literature predicts. The reform pathway requires no new theory. It requires political decision.

Three documented mechanisms: judicial reluctance when penalty is death · witness elimination incentive · family suppression when accused is known

Intervention 02
DNA Infrastructure Expansion
2 DNA machines for 170M people. 92% of 2025 samples unprocessed. 789 cases stalled. Minimum viable: 8 fully operational regional labs with 72-hour processing target.
Model: NPA South Africa regional forensic centres
Intervention 03
Witness Protection Enactment
Witness Protection Bill pending since 2015. Conviction requires witness. Witness requires protection. The Bill exists. Parliament needs to pass it. No new drafting required.
Status: Draft law exists — enactment only
Intervention 04
Mandatory Data Publication
PHQ annual conviction rates. Supreme Court trial compliance rates. NFDPL processing times. A single ministerial order mandating quarterly disclosure of 6 key metrics ends the data void at near-zero cost.
Cost: administrative — no new institution required
Intervention 05
Section 17 National Registry
No institution tracks §17 prosecutions. Under the 2025 expansion, monitoring is urgent. A court-level reporting requirement creates the first national registry. Required: Supreme Court administrative order.
Required: one administrative directive
