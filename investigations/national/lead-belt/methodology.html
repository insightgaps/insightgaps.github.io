01 · The Original Finding

What this investigation found that no prior report has.

The core finding of BD-INV-003 — "44 formally assessed lead-contaminated sites across Bangladesh have at least one school within 500 metres, placing an estimated 39,875 students in proximity to sites where soil lead has been measured at up to 1,702 times the international residential safety standard" — does not appear in any previously published document.

Prior research established that Bangladesh has a severe lead contamination crisis. Prior research identified and mapped contaminated sites. Prior research measured blood lead levels in children. No prior research calculated the number of schools or children within proximity to each assessed site. This spatial calculation is Insight Gaps' original contribution.

The investigation is the second school-proximity lead contamination analysis ever published for a developing country, after a 2016 study of 44 schools in Nairobi, Kenya (Journal of Health & Pollution, PMC6221501). It is the first for South Asia.

Sigma sentence: "44 formally assessed lead-contaminated sites across Bangladesh have at least one school within 500 metres — placing an estimated 39,875 students in proximity to sites where soil lead has been measured at up to 1,702 times the international safety standard. None have a public record of permanent closure." — Insight Gaps BD-INV-003, March 2026

03 · Spatial Analysis Method

The Haversine formula and the 500-metre buffer.

For each of the 294 contaminated sites in the Pure Earth database, Insight Gaps calculated the distance to every school node in the OpenStreetMap Bangladesh dataset. Schools within 500 metres of a site were counted and recorded.

Haversine Distance Formula
a = sin²(Δlat/2) + cos(lat₁) × cos(lat₂) × sin²(Δlon/2) c = 2 × atan2(√a, √(1−a)) d = R × c Where R = 6,371 km (Earth's mean radius) lat₁, lon₁ = contaminated site coordinates (Pure Earth) lat₂, lon₂ = school coordinates (OpenStreetMap) d = great-circle distance in km
The Haversine formula accounts for Earth's curvature and is the standard method for calculating distances between GPS coordinates. At Bangladesh latitudes (~23°N), the error compared to flat-earth calculation for 500m distances is approximately 0.01 metres — negligible.

Why 500 metres? The WHO residential soil contamination buffer standard uses 500m as the primary zone of concern for soil lead exposure pathways — direct ingestion, dust inhalation, and dermal contact. The icddr,b/Stanford 2025 study found significantly elevated BLL within 1km of industrial sites. The 500m buffer is conservative — sites within this radius represent the highest-risk proximity bracket.

The 59-metre finding: BD-4591 (ULAB Recycling Factory, Rosulpur, Kamrangir Char, Dhaka) to Rahmat Ali Ideal Govt. Primary School: 59.0 metres (Haversine calculation). GPS: Site 23.7227°N, 90.3698°E · School: nearest recorded node. This is the smallest site-to-school distance in the dataset.

04 · School Data

OpenStreetMap school nodes — query and extraction.

School locations were extracted from OpenStreetMap (OSM) using the Overpass API in March 2026. The query returned all features tagged amenity=school within the administrative boundary of Bangladesh.

Overpass Turbo Query (March 2026)
[out:json][timeout:90]; area["name"="Bangladesh"]["admin_level"="2"]->.searchArea; ( node["amenity"="school"](area.searchArea); way["amenity"="school"](area.searchArea); relation["amenity"="school"](area.searchArea); ); out center;

Result: 7,257 unique school nodes after deduplication (removing duplicate way/relation centroids). Only nodes with valid latitude and longitude were retained. OSM school data in Bangladesh is substantially complete for urban areas and government primary schools; rural coverage may be 60–80% complete.

Limitation: OSM is community-maintained and not an official registry. Schools that have opened after the last OSM edit in their area, or schools that have not been mapped, will not appear in this analysis. The true number of schools within 500m of contaminated sites may be higher than 145. The student count of 39,875 may be an underestimate.

05 · Enrollment Calculation

How we estimated 39,875 students — and why it replaced the original figure.

The original investigation used 400 students per school, sourced from BANBEIS national average figures. Research conducted for the v2 update found this significantly overstated enrollment.

275 students/school is the central estimate for government primary schools in urban Dhaka contexts. The 145 schools include institutions across all Bangladesh divisions, so a single urban Dhaka average may overstate for non-Dhaka schools. The conservative figure (24,650) and central estimate (39,875) are both cited in the investigation.

06 · Satellite Verification

How we confirmed 3 sites are still active — using Gemini Vision AI.

Because the Pure Earth assessment data is 7–10 years old (2016–2019), Insight Gaps conducted satellite imagery analysis of all 44 headline sites using Gemini Vision AI (gemini-2.0-flash-lite) in March 2026. This verifies whether operations are still ongoing at the time of publication.

Three sites were confirmed active as of March 2026: BD-4591 (Kamrangir Char, 59m from Rahmat Ali School), BD-4653 (Lalbagh), and BD-4802 (Jatrabari — confirmed active despite DoE enforcement drives in March–September 2025).

Exact Gemini Verification Prompt (used for all 44 sites)
"I am a data journalist. Site ID: [X]. GPS: [LAT, LON]. Is there visible evidence of industrial operations? Look for: corrugated structures, battery scrap piles, loading areas, vehicles, burn marks, workers, smoke. Answer: ACTIVE / INACTIVE / UNCERTAIN. Describe what you actually see. Confidence: HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW."

09 · Limitations

What this investigation cannot claim — and why it is still valid.

Data age (most significant limitation). The Pure Earth contamination data is 7–10 years old (assessed 2016–2019). Sites may have been cleaned, closed, or relocated since assessment. Satellite verification partially mitigates this — 3 sites confirmed still active in March 2026. 41 sites remain unverified. The finding that 44 sites were assessed with nearby schools remains valid regardless of current operational status; the regulatory failure to address them is itself the finding.

OSM school completeness. OpenStreetMap school coverage in rural Bangladesh may be 60–80% complete. The true count of schools within 500m of contaminated sites is likely higher than 145. The student estimate of 39,875 is therefore conservative for the schools component, even with the corrected enrollment figure.

Enrollment estimate. The 275 students/school central estimate applies to urban government primary schools. The 145 schools span multiple divisions and school types. Some schools in the dataset may be smaller madrasahs or community schools with lower enrollment; others in dense Dhaka urban areas may be larger. The range of 24,650–50,750 captures the uncertainty.

No independent soil tests. Insight Gaps has not commissioned independent laboratory testing at any site. All soil contamination figures are sourced from Pure Earth / University of Dhaka assessments (2016–2019). An independent soil test at BD-4591 (59m from Rahmat Ali school) would provide direct physical evidence at the most critical proximity point. This remains the single most important outstanding evidence gap.

No blood lead testing of school children. Insight Gaps has not tested any children for blood lead levels. The health impacts described are based on published peer-reviewed studies of population-level exposure in comparable contexts (Sultana et al. 2026, MICS 2025, Forsyth et al. 2021).

Satellite verification limitations. AI-assisted satellite analysis is indicative, not definitive. Imagery may not be current. Visual evidence of industrial activity does not constitute formal proof of lead contamination — only soil testing does.

These limitations do not invalidate the investigation's core finding. The proximity of 145 schools to 44 formally assessed contaminated sites — calculated with the Haversine formula from public datasets — is a factual spatial relationship that exists regardless of current site operational status. The finding that no public record exists of permanent regulatory closure of any of these sites is verifiable through the DoE records and news archive. The investigation's original contribution — the school-proximity calculation — stands.

10 · Replication Guide

How to replicate this analysis for any country.

This methodology is designed to be replicable globally wherever two data sources exist: a contaminated site database with GPS coordinates, and OpenStreetMap school data. The following steps reproduce the Bangladesh analysis for any country.

Step 1. Download contaminated site data from Pure Earth's TSIP database at contaminatedsites.org. Filter by country. Export as CSV with columns: site_id, lat, lon, max_soil_pb_ppm.

Step 2. Download school nodes from OpenStreetMap using the Overpass Turbo query in Section 04, substituting your country name. Export as GeoJSON.

Step 3. For each contaminated site, calculate the Haversine distance to every school node. Flag all schools within 500m (or your chosen buffer). Record: number of schools within buffer, closest school name, closest school distance.

Step 4. Apply national average school enrollment to estimate students at risk. Use official government statistics (equivalent of Bangladesh's APSC) rather than BANBEIS or older sources — enrollment figures vary significantly.

Step 5. Cross-reference regulatory records (equivalent of Bangladesh's DoE ECC database) to identify whether any sites near schools have been formally closed. This is the accountability finding.

# Python implementation of core Haversine calculation import math def haversine(lat1, lon1, lat2, lon2): R = 6371000 # Earth radius in metres phi1, phi2 = math.radians(lat1), math.radians(lat2) dphi = math.radians(lat2 - lat1) dlambda = math.radians(lon2 - lon1) a = (math.sin(dphi/2)**2 + math.cos(phi1) * math.cos(phi2) * math.sin(dlambda/2)**2) return 2 * R * math.atan2(math.sqrt(a), math.sqrt(1-a)) # Returns distance in metres between two GPS coordinate pairs # Usage: haversine(site_lat, site_lon, school_lat, school_lon)
