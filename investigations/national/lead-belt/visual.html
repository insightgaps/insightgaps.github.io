Scene 02 · The Harm

Lead does not warn you.
It is already permanent.

There is no safe level of lead in children. No antidote. No medication reverses the neurological damage. The only intervention that works is stopping the source before exposure happens.

🧠
IQ reduction — permanent
For every 10 μg/dL increase in blood lead, a child loses an estimated 1–5 IQ points. Permanently. Dhaka children have blood lead 4–6× the safe level. The intellectual loss compounds across an entire generation — it cannot be recovered.
⚡
Attention & behaviour
Lead exposure increases hyperactivity, impulsivity, and attention deficits. Children who cannot focus in class. Not a discipline problem. A poisoning problem. Caused by a factory the school can see from its playground.
❤️
Lifelong cardiovascular damage
Lead deposits in bones and releases into the bloodstream for decades. Adults poisoned as children have higher rates of hypertension and heart disease throughout their lives. The factory harms them in childhood. The body carries it forward.
🎓
Educational & economic loss
IQ loss and attention deficits translate into lower grades, lower wages, higher poverty rates. Bangladesh's annual economic loss from lead-related IQ reduction alone: USD 15.9 billion. Every year. Compounding. For children already in these schools.

Scene 02 · The Source

6,000 illegal recyclers.
6 are registered.

Used lead-acid batteries end up in backyard smelters. Lead melts in open air. Toxic residue soaks into soil beside homes, beside schools.

01 / 05
118,000

Metric tons of used lead-acid batteries generated in Bangladesh annually.

Every vehicle, generator, and UPS unit runs on a lead-acid battery. When it dies, it enters the recycling chain. Bangladesh generates 118,000 metric tons of used batteries every year — all requiring disposal or recycling. The infrastructure to handle this safely barely exists.

ILA 2020 · UNEP Assessment Report · CONFIRMED
02 / 05
30%

Only 30% reaches the formal sector — registered, inspected, licensed.

Six formally registered operators handle 30% of the battery stream. They hold environmental clearance certificates. They are subject to inspection. They employ workers with some level of safety oversight. Six companies. For a country of 170 million people.

UNCTAD / Pure Earth SMEP Programme 2024 · CONFIRMED
03 / 05
70%

70% goes to 6,000+ informal operations. No oversight. Open-air smelting. Toxic residue left in soil.

The majority of Bangladesh's batteries are broken open, melted in unventilated yards, and left to contaminate soil and groundwater. The workers — often children — breathe lead fumes. The residue stays for decades. This is not an accident. It is the infrastructure of the informal economy.

ESDO / UNEP Assessment · CONFIRMED

6

Formally registered battery recyclers. In all of Bangladesh.

The RJSC Bangladesh company registry confirms six registered formal operators against more than 6,000 informal. That is a 0.1% formalization rate. The Department of Environment is legally empowered to require Environmental Clearance Certificates from every single operation. It has not.

RJSC Bangladesh / Pure Earth / UNEP · CONFIRMED

Scene 05 · The Soil

How far above safe
is the ground?

International residential soil lead safety standard: 400 ppm. What Pure Earth measured at Bangladesh's sites goes orders of magnitude beyond that number.

This is the international benchmark for residential soil contamination. Children absorb lead from contaminated soil through hand-to-mouth contact during normal play. Every site in this investigation exceeds this standard. The question is by how much.

Scene 06 · The Children of Kathgora
21.3
micrograms per decilitre · median blood lead · children near Kathgora site
The factory had already stopped. The soil was being remediated. The children's blood lead was still 4.3 times the WHO safe level.

icddr,b and Stanford University researchers tested children living within 200 metres of the abandoned Kathgora site in Savar, 2019. Median blood lead: 21.3 μg/dL. Safe level: 3.5 μg/dL.


Pure Earth conducted soil remediation in 2019–2020. Soil lead dropped from a median of 1,400 mg/kg to 55 mg/kg. Seven months later: children's blood lead was still 17.0 μg/dL — 3.4 times safe. Lead had migrated from soil into dust, water, and food chains.


Stopping the source is necessary. It is not sufficient. The damage persists for years after operations stop. Every day these 6,000 sites continue operating adds years to the contamination footprint.

icddr,b / Stanford University · Environmental Research 2021 (open access) · CONFIRMED · BD-4921 · Pahloanpara Kathgora, Ashulia, Savar
