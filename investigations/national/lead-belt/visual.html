Scene 02 · The Child

Every morning, she walks through the gate. 59 metres away, the factory is burning.

Factory to school gate · Kamrangir Char, Dhaka

59m Factory to school gate — Kamrangir Char, Dhaka
825× Soil lead above safe limit at this single site
18 Schools within 500m of this one factory

A child attends a government primary school in Kamrangir Char, Dhaka. 59 metres from her school gate, a lead-acid battery recycling factory has been operating for over a decade. The lead in the surrounding soil is 825 times the international safe limit.

Her school does not know. Her parents do not know. The Department of Environment has known since 2016.

This is Site BD-4591 — Rosulpur, Kamrangir Char, Dhaka. Assessed by Pure Earth, June 2016. Satellite-confirmed active by Insight Gaps, March 2026. Soil lead: 330,001 ppm. The closest school — Rahmat Ali Ideal Govt. Primary School — sits 59 metres from the factory boundary.

Scene 06 · The Children of Kathgora

The factory had already stopped. The children's blood lead was still 4.3 times safe.

21.3

micrograms per decilitre · median blood lead · children near Kathgora site

icddr,b and Stanford researchers tested children within 200 metres of the abandoned Kathgora site in Savar, 2019. Median blood lead: 21.3 μg/dL. Safe level: 3.5 μg/dL.

After soil remediation: soil lead dropped from 1,400 mg/kg to 55 mg/kg. Seven months later: children's blood lead still 17.0 μg/dL — 3.4× safe. Lead had migrated into dust, water, and food chains.

Stopping the source is necessary. It is not sufficient. Every day these 6,000 sites continue operating adds years to the contamination footprint.

icddr,b / Stanford University · Environmental Research 2021 (open access) · PMID:33412099 Confirmed · BD-4921 · Pahloanpara Kathgora, Ashulia, Savar
