Scene 06 · The Children of Kathgora
21.3
micrograms per decilitre · median blood lead · children near Kathgora site
The factory had already stopped. The soil was being remediated. The children's blood lead was still 4.3 times the WHO safe level.

icddr,b and Stanford researchers tested children within 200 metres of the abandoned Kathgora site in Savar, 2019. Median blood lead: 21.3 μg/dL. Safe level: 3.5 μg/dL.

After soil remediation: soil lead dropped from 1,400 mg/kg to 55 mg/kg. Seven months later: children's blood lead still 17.0 μg/dL — 3.4× safe. Lead had migrated into dust, water, and food chains.

Stopping the source is necessary. It is not sufficient. Every day these 6,000 sites continue operating adds years to the contamination footprint.

icddr,b / Stanford University · Environmental Research 2021 (open access) · PMID:33412099 · Confirmed · BD-4921 · Pahloanpara Kathgora, Ashulia, Savar

Reckoning
22 years of documentation.
Zero permanent closures.

Six thousand illegal operations. 294 assessed contaminated sites. A national blood lead crisis costing 138,054 lives and $28.63 billion per year. The Department of Environment closed 28 sites in 2025 — none were among the 44 school-adjacent sites identified in this investigation. The gap is not knowledge. The gap is not authority. The gap is will.

Insight Gaps finding — BD-INV-003 · March 2026:

"44 formally assessed lead-contaminated sites across Bangladesh have at least one school within 500 metres — placing an estimated 39,875 students in proximity to sites where soil lead has been measured at up to 1,702 times the international residential safety standard. Based on the Pure Earth contaminated sites database and ESDO / UNEP reporting, Insight Gaps found no public record of a ULAB facility permanently closed by regulatory action in Bangladesh — based on DoE press releases, news archive 2010–2026, and RTI filed March 2026 · response pending."
