Scene 02 · The Harm

Lead does not warn you.
It is already permanent.

There is no safe level of lead in children. No antidote. No medication reverses neurological damage. The only intervention that works is stopping the source before exposure happens.

🧠
IQ reduction — permanent
For every 10 μg/dL increase in blood lead, a child loses an estimated 1–5 IQ points permanently. Dhaka children have blood lead 4–6× the safe level. The intellectual loss compounds across a generation.
⚡
Attention & behaviour
Lead exposure increases hyperactivity, impulsivity, and attention deficits. Not a discipline problem. A poisoning problem. Caused by a factory visible from the school playground.
❤️
Lifelong cardiovascular damage
Lead deposits in bones and releases into the bloodstream for decades. Adults poisoned as children have higher rates of hypertension and heart disease. The factory harms them in childhood. The body carries it forward.
🎓
$28.63 billion every year
Bangladesh's combined annual economic loss from lead: $10.89B from IQ reduction + $17.74B from cardiovascular deaths = $28.63 billion per year. Every year. For children already in these schools.
Lancet Planetary Health 2023: 138,054 premature cardiovascular deaths annually in Bangladesh caused by childhood lead exposure. Total annual cost: $28.63 billion. Bangladesh ranks 4th globally. DOI:10.1016/S2542-5196(23)00166-3 · PEER-REVIEWED

Scene 04 · The Source

6,000 illegal recyclers.
6 are registered.

Used lead-acid batteries end up in backyard smelters. Lead melts in open air. Toxic residue soaks into soil beside schools.

Every vehicle, generator, and UPS runs on a lead-acid battery. Bangladesh generates 118,000 metric tons of used batteries every year — all requiring disposal or recycling. The infrastructure to handle this safely barely exists.

Scene 08 · The Children of Kathgora
21.3
micrograms per decilitre · median blood lead · children near Kathgora site
The factory had already stopped. The soil was being remediated. The children's blood lead was still 4.3 times the WHO safe level.

icddr,b and Stanford researchers tested children within 200 metres of the abandoned Kathgora site in Savar, 2019. Median blood lead: 21.3 μg/dL. Safe level: 3.5 μg/dL.


After soil remediation: soil lead dropped from 1,400 mg/kg to 55 mg/kg. Seven months later: children's blood lead still 17.0 μg/dL — 3.4× safe. Lead had migrated into dust, water, and food chains.


Stopping the source is necessary. It is not sufficient. Every day these 6,000 sites continue operating adds years to the contamination footprint.

icddr,b / Stanford University · Environmental Research 2021 (open access) · CONFIRMED · BD-4921 · Pahloanpara Kathgora, Ashulia, Savar

Zero permanent closures.

Six thousand illegal operations. 270+ assessed contaminated sites. A national blood lead crisis costing 138,054 lives and $28.63 billion per year. Insight Gaps finding — BD-INV-003 · March 2026:

"44 formally assessed lead-contaminated sites across Bangladesh have at least one school within 500 metres — placing an estimated 39,875 students in proximity to sites where soil lead has been measured at up to 1,702 times the international residential safety standard. Based on the Pure Earth contaminated sites database and ESDO / UNEP reporting, no ULAB facility has a public record of permanent closure by regulatory action in Bangladesh."
