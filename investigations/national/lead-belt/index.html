The Investigation

6,000 illegal recyclers.
6 are registered.

Bangladesh generates approximately 100,000 metric tons of used lead-acid batteries annually. Seventy per cent of that stream flows to informal, unregulated operations — open-air smelting, no oversight, toxic residue left in soil that children then play on.

This investigation cross-referenced the Pure Earth contaminatedsites.org database of formally assessed contamination sites with OpenStreetMap school locations, calculating Haversine distances for all 294 Bangladesh entries against the WHO 500m residential standard.

The result is the first school-proximity lead analysis ever conducted for Bangladesh, and only the second such analysis globally for a developing country.
