01 / The Data Gap

THE REAL DEATH TOLL

Core Finding — Two Different Numbers, One Documented Gap
Bangladesh road fatalities are systematically undercounted. The WHO models approximately 31,578 deaths annually. Official government records (BRTA) report 5,480. The gap — approximately 5.8× — exists because deaths settled before police involvement, and hospital deaths not linked to crash records, never enter the official count. Sources: WHO Global Status Report on Road Safety 2023 · BRTA Annual Report 2023 · DGHS-CIPRB National Injury Survey 2023 · World Bank "Delivering Road Safety in Bangladesh" 2022

06 / Evidence-Based Solutions

DOCUMENTED INTERVENTIONS

Scope Notice
This section documents interventions with evidence of impact in comparable contexts. It does not predict outcomes for Bangladesh. Political feasibility assessment is outside this investigation's scope. Each recommendation is paired with its evidence base and transferability limitations.
#InterventionEvidence BaseComparatorStructural Barrier (Bangladesh)
S-01 Abolish Joma/Waybill System
Convert city bus drivers to monthly salary contracts. Eliminates the trip-based revenue target that directly incentivises racing.
 TransJakarta reform (2014): documented racing reduction post-salary conversion. Delhi DTC model. Dhaka Nagar Paribahan pilot (partial). Jakarta, Delhi Requires buy-in from owner class (92% politically connected per TIB). Franchise model needs enabling legislation.
S-02 Chassis-Linked Public Vehicle Registry
Permanent public record linking chassis/VIN to owner history, accident history, fitness certificates, route permits. Makes rebrand-and-continue documentable and traceable.
 Singapore LTA model. TransJakarta fleet system. UK DVLA accident linkage. Singapore, UK BRTA holds registration data. Public access is a policy decision, not a technical barrier. Requires political will.
S-03 Owner Accountability Under Road Transport Act 2018
When an unlicensed driver hired by an owner causes death, owner faces charge. The legal provision exists. Enforcement is absent.
 UK corporate manslaughter legislation. Australia heavy vehicle chain of responsibility. Road Transport Act 2018 (Bangladesh) — provision exists, documented unenforced. UK, Australia Legal framework partially exists. Prosecution requires judicial and enforcement independence.
S-04 Independent Accident Investigation Body
Separate from BRTA and police. Authority to publish findings. The Imad ARI-BUET investigation demonstrates what independent investigation can produce with current capacity.
 UK RAIB model. ARI-BUET Expressway investigation (Imad case) is the closest existing example in Bangladesh. UK, ARI-BUET ARI-BUET has investigative capacity. Requires legislative mandate and operational independence.

Five Confirmed Findings

  1. Bangladesh road deaths are systematically undercounted. WHO estimates ~31,578 deaths annually; BRTA official figure is 5,480. Gap: approximately 5.8× (PROBABLE — derived, model-dependent). Source: WHO Global Status Report 2023; BRTA Annual Report 2023.
  2. Six named operators are documented in connection with fatal incidents in Dhaka (2018–2025) across public records. Incidents are CONFIRMED where ≥2 independent sources exist. Ownership continuity claims are ALLEGED only.
  3. The waybill/joma system (TIB 2024) creates a documented structural incentive for bus racing. Drivers pay owners a fixed daily sum before earning their own wage. Source: TIB, "Integrity in Private Bus Transport Business," 2024.
  4. Bangladesh's estimated 102 deaths per 10,000 registered vehicles is the highest documented figure in South Asia (World Bank/ATO 2025). Fatality rate ~19.3 per 100,000 population places Bangladesh among worst-documented in Asia-Pacific. Source: WHO 2023; ATO 2025.
  5. Critical data — BRTA crash registry, hospital trauma records, police FIR operator linkage — does not exist in publicly accessible form. This absence is a documented accountability gap.

Methodology (3-sentence summary)

All claims are sourced from named public records: media archives, NGO research (TIB, BJKS, RSF), official statistics (WHO, BRTA, World Bank), and one official investigation report (ARI-BUET). Every claim carries an evidence tier label (CONFIRMED / PROBABLE / ALLEGED / UNVERIFIED) defined in the published Evidence Framework. No interviews were conducted; no outreach to named operators occurred prior to publication; this is a pattern documentation exercise, not a criminal or civil proceeding.

Methodology v1.0 · METH-001

HOW THIS REPORT WAS BUILT

This methodology is published so that any journalist, researcher, or auditor can: (a) understand how every claim was constructed; (b) identify its limitations; (c) replicate or extend the research; (d) evaluate the evidence tier of each claim.

M-001 · Data Sources
Source Hierarchy

Sources are ranked by evidential weight. Higher-ranked sources override lower-ranked sources where contradictions occur. All contradictions are logged in the dataset notes field.

  • Tier A — Primary Official: BRTA Annual Reports & enforcement notices · ARI-BUET published investigation reports · DGHS statistical bulletins · Court records cited in major press (with docket number or direct court quote) · WHO Global Status Report 2023 · World Bank published reports · Asian Transport Observatory Bangladesh Road Safety Profile 2025
  • Tier B — Primary NGO Research: TIB "Integrity in Private Bus Transport Business" 2024 · BJKS Annual Road Accident Reports 2018–2024 · RSF Dhaka Annual Report 2025 · CIPRB-DGHS National Injury Survey 2023
  • Tier C — Major Media Archives: The Daily Star · Prothom Alo · Dhaka Tribune · TBS News · The Financial Express (Bangladesh) · Daily Sun · Bangla Tribune · bdnews24
  • Tier D — Academic Literature: MIST and BUET conference proceedings · Peer-reviewed papers from ARI-BUET and partner institutions
  • Excluded: Anonymous tips or leaks · Social media posts without independent corroboration · Operator-produced content · Court documents not yet cited in verifiable press record

M-003 · Inclusion / Exclusion Rules
What Gets In, What Stays Out

INCLUDE: Any incident in the Dhaka Metropolitan Area (DMA) involving a scheduled city bus service resulting in at least one confirmed fatality, where the operator name or bus registration is stated in at least one verifiable source · BRTA enforcement actions regardless of whether a fatality occurred · National and Dhaka-specific road death statistics from named institutional sources

EXCLUDE: Incidents where operator is unidentified and registration is absent from all sources (noted in dataset) · Incidents where the bus was the struck party, not the striking party (assessed case-by-case; decision logged in notes) · Intercity incidents outside DMA boundary — Exception: Imad Paribahan included because ARI-BUET investigation formally documents BRTA fitness enforcement failure as a systemic finding · Social media only without verifiable press corroboration · Single paragraph with no victim name, registration, or court reference

Borderline cases: Reviewed against two sources before inclusion. Decision and rationale logged in DS-001 notes field.

M-004 · Deduplication Logic
Multiple Reports of the Same Incident

Multiple media reports of the same incident are treated as one data point in DS-001.

  • Match on incident date (exact or ± 1 day for overnight incidents)
  • Match on reported location (same street, intersection, or named landmark)
  • Match on reported victim name(s) where stated
  • Where victim names conflict, the source closest to the official record (police report/court document cited in press) is authoritative
  • Where victim names are absent: deduplication uses location + date + reported death count
  • All deduplication decisions logged in DS-001 notes field

INC-008 note: Cynthia Islam Mukta (Shahzadpur, same bus as INC-001) is logged as a separate incident despite same date and operator — different victim, different location, different moment. Decision is documented.

M-005 · Rebrand / Alias Handling
When Two Names Are Treated as a Sequence

A rebrand connection is recorded only when at least one of the following conditions is met:

  • (a) Same route operated under new name after enforcement action on original name — confirmed by two independent media sources
  • (b) A named investigative media report explicitly links old and new operator names with stated sourcing
  • (c) Eyewitness or victim family testimony states ownership continuity — recorded as testimony, not fact
  • (d) Same bus registration numbers appear under both entity names in verifiable records

The evidence tier of the rebrand link is assessed independently from the evidence tier of incidents associated with either entity. A confirmed fatality under a new operator name does not, by itself, confirm ownership continuity with a prior operator. See Phoenix Rulebook tab for full standards.

M-006 · Evidence Tier Assignment
How Tiers Are Applied

Evidence tiers are assigned to specific claims, not to source documents or operators as a whole. A single row in DS-001 may carry different tiers for different fields:

  • fatalities field: CONFIRMED (stated in 3 news reports)
  • operator_name_reported field: CONFIRMED (stated in court-cited press)
  • continuity_claim field: ALLEGED (eyewitness testimony only)

These are distinct claims with distinct tiers. Full definitions are in the Evidence Framework tab.

M-007 · Quantitative Claim Standards
How Numbers Are Presented
  • Point estimates derived from arithmetic on two sourced figures state both source figures and the calculation explicitly: WHO 31,578 ÷ BRTA 5,480 = 5.76×
  • Range estimates are preserved as ranges. Point estimates are not extracted from published ranges without explicit justification
  • Percentages include numerator, denominator, and source: "47% of victims were pedestrians (103 of 219, RSF 2025)" — not "47% of victims"
  • Extrapolations applying a rate from one geographic scope to another are labeled as inferences with stated limitations
  • WHO figures carry the badge "CONFIRMED (modelled estimate)" — confirmed as published, the underlying number is modelled, not enumerated
  • Derived ratios (e.g. 5.8×) carry "PROBABLE (derived, model-dependent)" — correct arithmetic inheriting the model uncertainty of both source figures
M-008 · Temporal Boundary Handling
Comparing Different Time Periods

Different source documents cover different time periods and geographic scopes. BJKS 2024 figures are national, 2024. RSF 2025 figures are Dhaka city only, 2025. WHO estimate is national, modelled for 2021. BRTA figures are national, 2023. These figures are not directly comparable. Cross-source comparisons are explicitly qualified in the report text and in dataset notes. No figure from one scope is presented as equivalent to a figure from a different scope without explicit qualification.

M-009 · Known Blind Spots
Documented Structural Bias
  • Media coverage bias: High-profile incidents in central Dhaka receive disproportionate coverage vs. peripheral areas, night incidents with few witnesses, and incidents settled before police involvement
  • Language access bias: English-language sources more thoroughly searched than Bangla. Incidents reported only in Bangla may be absent
  • No official BRTA crash registry: Dataset constructed entirely from secondary sources. Operator incident counts represent documented media-reported incidents, not a census
  • No hospital linkage: The 5.8× underreporting ratio is a modelled estimate, not hospital-verified data
  • Geographic ambiguity: Different sources use different definitions of "Dhaka" — City Corporation, Metropolitan Area, Division. Noted source-by-source but creates residual comparison uncertainty
  • No right-of-reply: No operator or authority was approached for comment prior to v1.0. Claims may be incomplete or one-sided in ways that operator input could correct
M-010 · Corrections & Versioning Policy
How Changes Are Handled

Version numbering: v1.x = minor updates (new data, tier adjustments, corrections). v2.0 = structural revision (new methodology section, map dataset upgrade, major data expansion).

Correction handling: Received → reviewed against source evidence within 14 days → if supported: applied and logged in CHANGELOG → if disputed: both original claim and submitted correction published side-by-side → no retroactive silent editing.

Dataset versioning: CSV files carry a dataset_version column and last_updated date. Previous versions are preserved, not deleted.

Anonymous submissions: Logged but not actioned without documentary support.

Evidence Framework v1.0 · EVF-001

EVIDENCE TIER DEFINITIONS

Every factual claim in this investigation carries one of four evidence tiers. This document defines each tier, sets minimum evidentiary thresholds, states mandatory qualification language, and specifies what each tier may and may not be used for. Badge display is mandatory — hover-only disclaimers do not meet this standard.

CONFIRMED ✓ Highest evidentiary standard

Minimum threshold (one of): ≥2 independent primary sources from different institutions or dates · 1 official/government source + 1 corroborating media record · Published court record, official government statistical publication, or formally published scientific investigation report with no credible contradiction

CONFIRMED does not mean: Beyond dispute · Legally established · Immune to new evidence. It means: publicly available evidence supports this claim sufficiently to assert it without qualification in body text.

Special badges (mandatory):

  • WHO figures → CONFIRMED (modelled estimate) — the figure is confirmed as published; the underlying number is modelled, not enumerated
  • Derived ratios → PROBABLE (derived, model-dependent) — correct arithmetic inheriting model uncertainty of both source figures

May be used for: Body text assertions · Section headings · Summary statistics · Headlines (with source attribution)

Still requires: Source attribution in text ("WHO estimates...") · Cannot be used to establish criminal guilt, corporate ownership, or intent

PROBABLE ◐ Pattern-level confidence

Minimum threshold (one of): 1 primary source with no contradiction + corroborating documented pattern · Direct logical inference from 2+ CONFIRMED claims · Named investigative journalism report with described sourcing consistent with corroborating patterns

Mandatory language in text: "Available evidence suggests..." · "Records indicate..." · "The documented pattern is consistent with..."

May be used for: Pattern analysis · Structural findings · Section-level arguments with stated qualifier

Must NOT be used for: Headlines without qualification · Ownership assertions · Intent assertions · Any claim attributed to a specific named individual without corroboration

ALLEGED ? Requires explicit qualification + source naming

Mandatory when: Eyewitness testimony · Single-source press without described methodology · Intent or motive claims · Ownership claims without corporate registry evidence · Right-of-reply not sought (applies throughout this release)

Source of allegation must always be named. This report does not publish anonymous allegations.

Three visually distinct sub-classes — must not share the same badge:

👁 EYEWITNESS ACCOUNT Sub-class A of ALLEGED

Definition: Direct first-person witness to the incident itself. Present at scene. Saw the crash. Saw the racing.

Example: Witnesses who saw the two Akash Paribahan buses racing on Progati Sarani, Madhya Badda, before Tasnim Jahan Airin was struck — as reported in TBS News and Dhaka Tribune (October 2024).

Epistemic status: Higher reliability than Sub-class B or C for incident facts. Cannot establish ownership, corporate identity, or intent.

○ FAMILY / VICTIM TESTIMONY Sub-class B of ALLEGED

Definition: Statement from a victim's family member or the victim about their belief regarding ownership, corporate identity, or continuity between named operators. They were not necessarily present at the incident. They are asserting belief, not observation.

Example: Badda victim family's belief that Akash Paribahan, Victor Classic, and Suprobhat share the same owner — as reported by TBS News and Dhaka Tribune (October 2024).

Critical distinction: This is a different epistemic category from Sub-class A. It must never be visually grouped with Eyewitness Account. A family member's belief about corporate ownership does not constitute evidence of corporate ownership.

⊡ SINGLE-SOURCE PRESS Sub-class C of ALLEGED

Definition: Claim from one media report whose sourcing methodology is not described in the accessible version of the article.

Example: Daily Sun (April 2019) investigative report linking Suprobhat Paribahan to Victor Classic as a rebrand — key source for the Phoenix Hypothesis, but the sources used by the Daily Sun reporter are not described in the accessible text of the article.

Citation requirement: The original source must be named as the attribution ("Daily Sun reported..."), not this dataset. This sub-class carries the highest legal risk of the three — external users should consult their own legal counsel before publishing claims carrying this badge.

— UNVERIFIED Gap documentation only

When used: Data classified or not publicly accessible · Available sources contradict each other without resolution · Required dataset does not exist in public form

UNVERIFIED means: This investigation cannot confirm or deny the claim using available open sources. The claim may be true. The label documents an evidentiary gap, not a finding of falsehood.

May be used for: Documenting data gaps · Identifying where future investigation is needed

Must NOT be used for: Supporting any other claim · Pattern analysis · Comparative assessment

External Citation Rule — Mandatory
When citing a specific claim from this dataset, the evidence tier for that claim must be reproduced alongside the citation. Omitting the tier label when citing PROBABLE, ALLEGED, or UNVERIFIED claims misrepresents the certainty of the underlying claim and violates the attribution requirement of the CC BY-NC 4.0 license under which this work is published.

L-002 · What This Publication Does NOT Assert

Phoenix Rulebook v1.0 · PHX-001

PHOENIX HYPOTHESIS — EVIDENCE STANDARDS

Framing Notice — Mandatory
The operator sequence documented in this investigation (Suprobhat → Victor Classic → Akash) is a pattern hypothesis derived from public records. It does not assert that named entities are legally identical, under common ownership, or controlled by the same individuals. Each link carries an evidence tier label that defines the confidence level of that specific connection.
PX-001 · Ownership Continuity Claims
What evidence is required to claim same ownership

CONFIRMED requires: Same individual named in BRTA permit records for both entities · OR same individual named in RJSC (Bangladesh company registry) documents for both entities · OR court documents from proceedings on both entities naming the same person · Two independent documents from different source classes required.

PROBABLE requires: Named investigative journalism report states same ownership with described sourcing AND route continuity independently confirmed AND no credible source contradicts

ALLEGED is mandatory when: Only source is eyewitness testimony or victim/family statement · Source is a single media report that did not describe its sourcing methodology

Forbidden — no exception: "Company X and Y are the same company" · "Owner [name] controls both entities" · "Company X is a front for Company Y"

Current status (Suprobhat/Victor/Akash chain): Family/victim testimony — victim family stated belief in ownership continuity. RJSC search not completed as of v1.0. Corporate registry confirmation: absent.

PX-002 · Route Continuity Claims
What evidence is required to claim same route

CONFIRMED requires: BRTA route permit records showing same route assigned to new entity · OR two independent media sources, published on different dates, reporting new entity operating same route after enforcement action on prior entity

PROBABLE requires: One media source reports new entity on same route AND timing is consistent with enforcement action (new entity appears within 6 months)

Current status (Sadarghat–Gazipur / Progati Sarani corridor): PROBABLE — Daily Star archive and Daily Sun (2019) both report Victor Classic operating same corridor following March 2019 Suprobhat ban. Victor Classic to Akash corridor continuity: PROBABLE (media reports, single source for name-link).

PX-003 · Vehicle Continuity Claims
What evidence is required to claim same physical buses

CONFIRMED requires: Same chassis number or vehicle registration in verifiable records under both entity names

PROBABLE requires: Authenticated photographic evidence showing same physical bus under different livery, with image authentication performed

Current status: UNVERIFIED — BRTA vehicle registry not publicly accessible. No authenticated photographic evidence in this dataset. Vehicle continuity may not be claimed.

PX-004 · Intent Claims
What evidence is required to claim deliberate evasion

CONFIRMED requires: Direct statement on record by individual acknowledging intent · OR internal communications establishing intent (documentary evidence) · OR court finding of deliberate evasion

FORBIDDEN — absolutely no exception: "Operators deliberately rebranded to escape accountability" · "The rebrand was designed to evade prosecution" · "Name change was done in bad faith" · "Owners laundered their corporate identity"

Permissible language at current evidence level: "The documented sequence shows enforcement action followed by operation under a new name on the same route. The motivations behind this sequence are not established by available evidence."

Current status: UNPUBLISHABLE as an intent claim. Pattern consistent with evasion but intent not established. No documentary evidence of intent exists in this dataset.
