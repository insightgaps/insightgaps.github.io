Scene 01 · The Gap

Two Numbers. One Country. One Counting Choice.

Watch the official tally. Then watch what the data actually shows.

Step 01 of 05

Every year. The same roads. Two different answers.

Bangladesh has two road death counts. They are not in dispute. They measure different things. The government counts only what police record. The WHO models what actually happens to human bodies on these roads.

WHO Global Status Report 2023 · BRTA Annual Report 2023
Step 02 of 05
5,480

The BRTA official count.

Police FIR records compiled by the Bangladesh Road Transport Authority. Acknowledged limitation: many crashes are settled before police are called. Deaths that happen in hospital days after a crash are rarely linked back. This number counts what enters the formal record.

BRTA Annual Report 2023 · CONFIRMED
Step 03 of 05
8,543

The BJKS media scan. 37 national newspapers.

The Bangladesh Jatri Kalyan Samity scanned 37 daily newspapers in 2024 and tallied every reported road death. 8,543. Already 55% higher than BRTA. But newspapers also miss what happens in villages at night, in unreported incidents, in hospital wards.

BJKS Annual Report 2024 · CONFIRMED (media-scan methodology)
Step 04 of 05
~31,578

The WHO modelled estimate.

WHO applies a corrective model using hospital injury data, population surveys, and mortality registries. The result: approximately 31,578 road deaths per year in Bangladesh — nearly six times the official count.

WHO Global Status Report on Road Safety 2023 · DGHS-CIPRB National Injury Survey 2023 · CONFIRMED (modelled)
Step 05 of 05
~5.8×

The gap is a counting choice, not a data problem.

Hospital records exist. Crash scene records exist. The link between them does not — by policy, not by technical incapacity. The BRTA holds registration data. The DGHS holds mortality data. No data-sharing protocol requires them to be connected. What is not counted cannot be held accountable.

PROBABLE (derived, model-dependent) · World Bank "Delivering Road Safety in Bangladesh" 2022
Scene 02 · The Machine

Why Buses Race — The Economic Architecture of Death

Scroll through the mechanism. Racing is not recklessness. It is the rational output of a designed system.

🏢 Bus Owner
Tk 4,000–6,000 / day
Fixed daily joma (জমা) collected regardless of trips completed. Owner's income is guaranteed. All operational risk transfers to the driver and helper team.
↓ joma is paid first ↓
📋 Waybill Checker
Checkpoint system
Owner-employed checkers count passengers at stops along the route. The first bus to reach each stop takes all waiting passengers. First arrival wins the fare.
↓ only surplus becomes wage ↓
🚌 Driver / Helper
~Tk 300–500 / day net
Driver pays joma first. Then fuel. Only what remains becomes their wage. Racing and overloading are not recklessness — they are rational survival strategies under this structure.
↓ racing is rational ↓
⚠ Racing on Public Roads
Pedestrian risk
Two same-route buses race stop-to-stop. The October 2024 Badda incident is a documented output of this structure. The system produced it. It will produce it again.
↓ enforcement bypassed ↓
🏛 Political Shield
92% linked — TIB 2024
TIB (2024) documents 92% of operators with political connections (single source — cited as TIB estimate). Tk 1,059 crore/year in bribery flows through the sector. Blacklisted operators continue appearing in incident records.
Step 01 of 05

Start with the owner. The owner is not on the bus.

The owner collects a fixed daily sum called joma — between Tk 4,000 and 6,000 per day. This is collected regardless of how many passengers were carried, how fast the bus drove, or whether anyone died. The economic risk of the road does not touch the owner.

TIB "Integrity in Private Bus Transport Business" 2024 · CONFIRMED (attributed TIB)
Step 02 of 05
Tk 4,000–6,000

Per day. Guaranteed. Before the driver earns a taka.

The waybill checker is the enforcement mechanism of this system — an owner-employed counter at bus stops who records which bus arrived first and collected the passengers. The race is the business model.

TIB 2024 · CONFIRMED (attributed TIB)
Step 03 of 05
~Tk 300–500

What the driver earns. After joma. After fuel.

Racing is not a character flaw. It is the correct response to the incentive structure. A driver who drives safely and arrives second earns nothing meaningful. A driver who races earns a wage. The structure produces the behavior.

TIB 2024 · PROBABLE (single source)

Scene 06 · The Voids

Four Datasets. Each One Held by the Government. None Published.

Each missing dataset is not a research limitation. It is a documented accountability gap.

VOID-01
BRTA Bus Crash Registry
by vehicle chassis
Would confirm or deny Phoenix ownership chains definitively. Would enable operator-level death rate calculation. BRTA holds it. Classified. Not published.
VOID-02
Hospital Trauma Records
linked to crash cause
The only ground truth for the 5.8× undercount claim. Hospital deaths are not linked to road crash records. Paper records, undigitised, unconnected. DGHS holds them. Not aggregated.
VOID-03
Police FIR linked
to operator name
FIRs record registration numbers, not operator brands. A single recording policy change — no new infrastructure required — would make operator accountability possible at scale. Not implemented.
VOID-04
Route permit beneficial
owner registry
Many permits held by front companies or politically connected individuals. Publication would expose political ownership of the bus sector. Classified. RTI application possible — politically sensitive.

Four datasets the government holds. Four datasets that do not require new technology to publish. Four decisions not to.

This is not a technical limitation. This is a political choice.

S-01 · Reform
Abolish the Joma / Waybill System
Convert city bus drivers to monthly salary contracts. Eliminates the trip-based revenue target that directly incentivises racing. Requires owner class buy-in (92% politically connected per TIB). Franchise model needs enabling legislation.
Evidence: TIB 2024 structural analysis
S-02 · Reform
Chassis-Linked Public Vehicle Registry
Link chassis/VIN to owner history, accident history, fitness certificates, and route permits in a public database. Makes rebrand-and-continue documentable. BRTA already holds the data. Publication is a policy decision.
Models: Singapore LTA · UK DVLA · TransJakarta
S-03 · Enforcement
Owner Accountability Under Road Transport Act 2018
When an unlicensed driver hired by an owner causes death, the owner faces charge. The legal provision exists. Enforcement is documented as absent. Requires prosecutorial and judicial independence.
RTA 2018 provision confirmed · enforcement gap documented
S-04 · Institution
Independent Accident Investigation Body
Separate from BRTA and police. Authority to publish findings. The ARI-BUET Imad case investigation demonstrates what independent investigation can produce with current capacity — the only official published investigation in this dataset.
Model: UK RAIB · ARI-BUET precedent
