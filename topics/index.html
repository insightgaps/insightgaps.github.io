How Research Programs Work

Each program defines a domain of systemic failure and publishes investigations, datasets, and analysis under that mandate. Investigations within a program are related — they share methodologies, build on each other's findings, and collectively build toward a comprehensive picture of a systemic problem. Programs expand as new investigations are published. New programs are added when sufficient evidence justifies a new research domain.

Commissioned Research

Private research engagements accepted across all program areas. Same forensic standards, evidence tiers, and methodology as public work. Results are confidential.
