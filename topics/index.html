Research Programs

Structured investigation domains. Each program clusters related investigations, datasets, and analysis under a defined research mandate — building systematic knowledge, not isolated articles.

How Research Programs Work

Each program defines a domain of systemic failure and publishes investigations, datasets, and analysis under that mandate. Investigations within a program are related — they share methodologies, build on each other's findings, and collectively build toward a comprehensive picture of a systemic problem. Programs expand as new investigations are published.

Commissioned Research

Private research engagements accepted across all program areas. Same forensic standards, evidence tiers, and methodology as public work. Results are confidential.
