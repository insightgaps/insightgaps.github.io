BD-INV-002 · Published March 2026 · v1.6 Public Release

The Impunity Machine: Bangladesh's Rape Justice Crisis

A forensic audit of Bangladesh's rape justice system. 310 convictions in 23 years for 66,711 women who reached a One-Stop Crisis Centre. Zero convictions in the first seven months of 2024. Documents the structural chain producing this outcome: silence, shalish, forensic failure, delay, acquittal.

Primary Findings
  • 0.46% conviction rate: Of 66,711 women who reached a crisis centre (2001–Jul 2024), only 310 resulted in conviction.
  • Zero convictions (Jan–Jul 2024): 643 women sought help. 227 cases filed. 12 verdicts. Not one conviction.
  • Death penalty failure: Three mechanisms by which the 2020 amendment likely worsened, not improved, conviction rates.

BD-INV-001 · Published February 2026 · v1.0 Public Release

Blood Routes: The Dhaka Bus Fatalities Investigation

An open-source forensic dataset on bus-related fatalities in Dhaka, the structural incentives of the waybill (Joma) system, and the accountability failures that perpetuate them. Six operators documented. Phoenix hypothesis: banned operators rebranding to evade enforcement.

Primary Findings
  • 5.8× data gap: Official figures undercount fatalities by approximately 5.8× vs WHO estimates.
  • Phoenix phenomenon: Banned operators (e.g. Suprobhat) rebrand as Victor Classic and Akash to bypass enforcement.
  • Joma system: The waybill system creates rational economic incentives for bus racing, directly causing pedestrian deaths.
