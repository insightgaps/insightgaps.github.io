The Hidden Tax of War: How Conflicts Raise Global Prices

How armed conflicts — Iraq 2003, Arab Spring 2011, Ukraine 2022, Red Sea 2023–24 — transmit economic shocks through energy, food, and shipping markets to countries that never participated. Introduces the War Externality Index (WEI) framework for comparative cross-conflict analysis.

Primary Findings
  • One repeated transmission mechanism across multiple conflicts drives global price spillovers to uninvolved nations.
  • Import-dependent countries absorb disproportionate welfare losses — bottom-quintile households lose 3–5% of real income.
  • The WEI framework enables cross-conflict quantitative comparison of economic externalities for the first time.
