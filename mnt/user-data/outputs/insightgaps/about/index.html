About

InsightGaps

We find the gaps in official narratives — and document them with data.

InsightGaps is a forensic data journalism project based in Bangladesh. We investigate what official records omit, what statistics obscure, and what narratives don't survive contact with primary source data.

The project was founded on a single conviction: that the most consequential stories in Bangladesh are not being told because they require sustained data work that traditional newsrooms don't have the time or structure for. InsightGaps exists to do that work.

Our investigations begin with a gap — something the official record should contain but doesn't, or something the official record claims but the underlying data contradicts. We then build original datasets from primary sources: court records, RTI requests, NGO case registers, on-record interviews. We publish the data alongside the findings so others can check our work.

The Impunity Machine investigation is the first major output of this project. It produced the only independent registry of rape conviction outcomes in Bangladesh — a dataset spanning 23 years, 66,711 women, and 310 convictions. No government body had compiled this. We did.

AH Anik is the founder of InsightGaps and a journalism student at the University of Chittagong, Department of Communication & Journalism. All work published under CC BY-NC 4.0.

Site Rules
10 Rules We Follow Without Exception
  • Never publish a figure that isn't tied to a named source or primary document.
  • If the data shows something surprising, dig harder. Not an excuse to rush.
  • Survivors are not case studies. Every data point is a person.
  • We undercount, not overcount. If the real number is worse, our direction holds.
  • Corrections are permanent, visible, and dated. We do not silently update.
  • Official denial is data, not a rebuttal. We publish both.
  • We do not accept advertising. We do not take money that shapes coverage.
  • Source protection is non-negotiable. Consent is explicit and ongoing.
  • The full dataset is always available for inspection. Findings must be reproducible.
  • We stop publishing when we can't verify. A gap in publication is better than a gap in fact.
